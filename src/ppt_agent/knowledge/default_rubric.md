# Presentation / deck evaluation (observe only extracted slide or page text)

Use what appears in the document text below. You do **not** see fonts, animations, speaker notes, or layout unless that text is present in the extract. If something cannot be inferred from the extract, score conservatively and say so in the comment.

Scoring uses the **built-in criterion rows** (A1–F2) supplied alongside this text in the model prompt—**not** organizer-defined rubrics.

Score each row as an **integer 0–5** (where 0 = no evidence / not inferable from extracted text, 5 = excellent).

## A. Submission and format
- **A1** Document is readable: slides or pages have substantive text (not blank or placeholder-only).
- **A2** Project and team are identifiable early if the course requires it (titles, headers, or first slides).
- **A3** File type is appropriate (.pptx deck or PDF); content maps to slides/pages consistently.

## B. Structure and narrative
- **B1** Logical flow (problem/context → approach → results or demo → conclusion or next steps).
- **B2** Sections or slide titles support the story; ordering makes sense.
- **B3** Appropriate depth: not only buzzwords; enough detail to follow the argument.

## C. Technical and product content
- **C1** Core features, architecture, or methodology are described concretely (not only names).
- **C2** If integrations or external systems matter, they are explained or scoped, or gaps are acknowledged.
- **C3** Limitations, mocks, or unfinished areas are disclosed when relevant.

## D. Clarity for the audience
- **D1** Terminology is defined or used consistently; acronyms explained when needed.
- **D2** Diagrams or lists are referenced in text enough to understand intent (even without seeing the image).
- **D3** Takeaways or conclusions are explicit where expected for the assignment type.

## E. Integrity
- **E1** Claims in the deck match the level of detail shown (no overstated “done” without support in text).
- **E2** Repo, demo, or video links are mentioned only if they appear in the extract.

## F. Optional
- **F1** Reproducibility or setup notes if the course requires them.
- **F2** Timeline, risks, or teamwork only if present and relevant.

When the extract is thin (e.g., images without OCR text), note that limits in the criterion comments and avoid inventing content that is not in the document.
